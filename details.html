    Supplier Name:

    The supplier name in an NCR report identifies where non-conforming materials or services originated.

    Production Number:

    The Product Number identifies the specific item or material associated with the Non-Conformance Report (NCR), ensuring precise tracking and resolution of the issue.

    Quantity Received:

    Quality received indicates the total quantity of items received by the Quality department for inspection as part of the NCR process.

    Forward to Engineering:

    Indicates that the Non-Conformance Report (NCR) or issue needs to be sent to the Engineering department for further review, evaluation, and determination of corrective actions.

    Applicable Process:

    The applicable process in an NCR report identifies the specific step where the non-conformance occurred, helping to pinpoint causes and implement targeted corrective actions for improved quality.

    Sales Order Number:

    The Sales Order Number identifies the specific customer order associated with this Non-Conformance Report (NCR). It helps link the issue to the original order for accurate tracking and resolution.

    Quantity Defective:

    Quantity Defective Represents the quantity of items found to be defective or non-conforming during the Quality inspection.

    Item marked conforming:

    Indicates that the item has passed inspection and meets all required standards and specifications. It is deemed acceptable for use or delivery without further action needed.

    Item Description:

    The Item description provides a detailed description of the product or material associated with the Non-Conformance Report (NCR) to clearly identify the item in question.

    Description of Defect:

    Description of defect provides a detailed explanation of the issue or problem found with the product or material during the inspection. This helps clearly define the nature of the defect for corrective actions and follow-up.

      Uploaded Images/Videos:

      Allows you to attach images or videos related to the defect or non-conformance. This can include photos of the defective items or videos demonstrating the issue, providing visual evidence to support the NCR.

      Review By CF Engineering:

      Choose a resolution to define how the non-conformance will be addressed, ensuring proper documentation and compliance with quality standards.

      Does Customer require notification of NCR?

      This field indicates whether the customer should be notified about the non-conformance. Select "Yes" if the customer needs to be informed, or "No" if no notification is required.

      Disposition:

      The action taken to resolve the non-conformance, such as "Use As Is," "Repair," "Rework," or "Scrap." This decision determines how the non-conforming item will be handled.

      Does the drawing require updating?

      Indicates whether the drawing for the product or component needs to be revised due to the non-conformance. Select "Yes" if an update is needed, or "No" if the drawing remains unchanged.

      Original Revision Number:

      This refers to the revision number of the original drawing or design before any updates or changes were made. This helps track the version of the design being referenced

      Updated Revision Number:

      This refers to the revision number assigned to the drawing or design after updates or changes have been made. This helps track the most current version of the design following any modifications.

      Revision Date:

      The date when the drawing or design was last updated or revised. This helps track when changes were made to ensure the most current version is used.

      Name of Engineer:

      The name of the engineer responsible for reviewing or making updates to the drawing or design. This helps identify the person who made the revisions or decisions regarding the non-conformance.
